The Concept

FoldsVTG started as a question: what if every garment you wore had a reason? Not a marketing reason — a real one. Vintage clothing is the most honest clothing. It already lived a life. It has texture. It came from somewhere.

The project sits between curation and design — sourcing vintage pieces with specific character, and reworking or pairing them with original garment designs that share the same sensibility. The result is a wardrobe that tells a story, piece by piece.

The Work

Curation is the core skill. Finding pieces that carry something — a history, a cut, a fabric weight — that contemporary mass production can't replicate. The eye for this develops slowly, through years of looking at clothes as objects rather than commodities.

Original designs within FoldsVTG borrow from that same vocabulary. Heavy fabrics, considered construction, details that reward attention. Graphics are minimal and purposeful — typography or mark-making that adds meaning rather than noise.

Editorial direction shapes how the clothing is presented. Natural light, unforced styling, environments that have personality. The photography is not aspirational in the traditional sense — it is documentary. Here is the thing. Here is where it lives.

Why It Matters

FoldsVTG is a counterpoint to speed. It is slow, deliberate, and specific. In a market where brands launch and collapse in cycles, longevity comes from having a genuine point of view — not a strategy.

Every piece released is something worth keeping. That is the standard.
