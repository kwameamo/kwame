The Brief

FooseLine Fest needed a brand that could carry the energy of the event before anyone stepped through the door. Events live and die by first impressions — the poster, the ticket, the social post. Everything that reaches the audience before the experience begins.

The brief was to build a visual identity bold enough to stop scrolling, clear enough to communicate at a glance, and flexible enough to work across all the surfaces an event demands — digital, print, signage, merchandise.

The Work

The identity leads with a strong, kinetic mark — energy and motion built into the letterforms, communicating competition and spectacle without being literal. It works in black and white as cleanly as it does in the full colour system.

The colour palette was designed for impact: high-contrast combinations that read well on screens and hold up in print. Typography is purposeful — a bold display face for headlines, a clear secondary for information hierarchy.

Event collateral was designed as a complete system: posters, digital banners, ticket design, wristbands, and social templates. Every application was considered against the real-world environment it would live in.
