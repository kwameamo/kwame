The Brief

The food and dining space is saturated. Brands that look the same, feel the same, communicate nothing distinct. ChopRight came in with a clear personality — direct, unpretentious, confident — and needed a visual identity that matched that voice.

The brief was simple: build a brand that doesn't look like every other food brand. Something that has character, feels local without being cliché, and works across every format from packaging to social media.

The Work

The logo is direct — no decoration that doesn't earn its place. Strong letterforms, deliberate weight, a mark that holds at any size. It reads confident rather than loud.

The colour system is warm and grounded. Not the default red-and-yellow of the category — instead, tones that feel appetite-forward without being generic. Paired with a type system that balances personality and clarity.

Brand guidelines cover every application — from packaging labels to digital banners to staff print materials — with clear rules for how the identity behaves in each context. The goal is a brand that stays coherent as it grows.

The Outcome

ChopRight has an identity that matches its ambition. A brand that feels ready to grow — into new locations, new products, new audiences — without needing a rebrand to do it. The foundation is solid.
