The Brief

Foundations carry the burden of institutional trust in a way commercial brands don't. Donors, partners, and beneficiaries all need to believe in the organisation before they engage with it. The identity has to do that work quietly — not through decoration, but through design decisions that signal stability, seriousness, and purpose.

The brief was to create a logo and identity system for the Akrofi Bene Est Foundation that would represent the organisation with appropriate weight — across official documents, stationery, signage, and digital presence.

The Work

The logo is built for longevity. Institutional marks don't chase trends — they need to look as valid in ten years as they do today. The mark combines a distinctive symbol with a clean wordmark, reading equally well at the scale of a letterhead header and a building sign.

The colour system is authoritative without being corporate. A strong primary palette, carefully paired with secondary tones that allow flexibility across foundation materials. Every decision was made against the context: these are official documents, formal correspondence, and public-facing materials.

Stationery and collateral — letterhead, envelopes, business cards, and document templates — were designed as a complete system. The foundation presents consistently across every formal touchpoint.

The Outcome

The Akrofi Bene Est Foundation now has an identity that matches the seriousness of its work. The logo communicates purpose and credibility from first sight. The brand system is complete and ready to be applied consistently across all formal and public-facing contexts.
