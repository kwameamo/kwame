The Idea

Vision'97 was not built to be a brand. It was built to be a statement. The name comes from a year, a place, a starting point — and the belief that where you start does not determine where you end up. That is the entire brief. Everything else is execution.

Streetwear is a saturated category. The only way to build something meaningful in it is to have a genuine point of view and the patience to develop it properly. Vision'97 is built for the long game.

The Work

The identity is built around a mark that reads as both personal and universal — the kind of thing someone would want to wear without needing to explain. Clean, confident, with enough tension to be interesting.

Garment design prioritises construction over decoration. Cuts that fit deliberately. Fabrics that have weight. Graphics that say something rather than fill space. The goal is clothing that improves with wear, not clothing that peaks on release day.

Cut-and-sew pieces are designed with specific proportions — oversized where it should be oversized, fitted where structure matters. Every silhouette is considered against how it will actually be worn, not just how it photographs.

Art direction for drops follows a consistent visual language: specific environments, natural light, real people. No excess. The clothing should be the most interesting thing in the frame.

Where It Stands

Vision'97 is in active development. The brand exists, the identity is set, and the first drop is being prepared with the discipline that the brand demands — small quantities, considered execution, no compromises on quality to hit a date.

The strategy is patience. Building a brand worth wearing takes longer than building a brand worth buying. Vision'97 is built for the former.
